Explanation

There are three documents that govern and set the rules of RAHU Western Australia

RAHU Constitution

The constitution can be viewed here.

The RAHU Constitution represents the highest, peak of rules of all RAHU. Any rule written in the RAHU Constitution cannot be over-ruled. The constitution can only be amended at a “Annual General Meeting.” This meeting is open to all RAHU members, and for any changes to the constitution to be passed 75% of members present at the AGM must vote for the change.

RAHU General Bylaws

The General Bylaws can be viewed here.

RAHU General Bylaws are bylaws set by the Governing Committee. They can be changed at any Governing Committee meeting with a standard majority vote. These rules will over-ride and take precedent over local branch bylaws.

WA Branch Bylaws

The RAHU WA Bylaws can be viewed here.

The WA branch sets local by laws. Any branch can set the bylaws, and these bylaws can be changed at any RAHU WA branch meeting with a simple motion and majority vote
